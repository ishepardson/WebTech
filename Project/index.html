Tempelhof

During my semester abroad, I lived in Tempelhof, a neighborhood in Berlin. I lived across from the decommissioned Tempelhof Airport with my host mother Elke. I decided to do a homestay because I wanted to become more involved with the culture and learn more about my environment and the people around me. Elke was able to help me with my German, help me try new local foods, introduce me to different forms of art, teach me about the nation's politics and expose me to new ways of thinking. Because of my homestay, I was able to learn an immense amount of information and adapt a new way of thinking. Now I approach many things in life with a more open mind and I now want to learn so much more about the many other cultures across the globe.

IES Abroad

I studied in Berlin through the IES Abroad program. The IES center was located in the neighborhood Mitte in the center of Berlin. There I took 5 different classes: German language, Metropolitan Studies, Visual Culture and the Urban Landscape, Metropolitan Development, and Sex, Drugs, and Rock 'N Roll: The Impacts of Pop-Culture on the Modern Metropolis. Through this program, I was able to fill most of my liberal arts requirments while gaining a totally new perspective on global issues. I chose this program because it was the only program in Berlin that offered the homestay option. I know that the homestay option is not for everyone, but I highly suggest it to anyone who is interested in becoming fully immersed within their host country's culture. On top of this, I recognized that much of IES' curriculum was taught out in the field as each course I took utilized field trips as a way to learn experientially.
